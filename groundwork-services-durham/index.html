The three structural changes most likely to move the needle

Top 3

Not solutions, the changes themselves. On the diagnostic Medhya confirms which of these are live for you, on your leaders' own answers, and what each one is worth.

Set clear decision rights across the three divisions

Civil engineering, sports surfaces and North East Tippers each need defined authority so that site and operational decisions stop routing back to Michael, Jon and Kim. When three directors are the decision bottleneck for 173 people across three business lines, senior time is spent on calls that should sit a level down.

Standardise how work flows between the divisions

Jobs that touch both the earthworks arm and the haulage arm need one agreed handover process. Without it, work gets coordinated by chasing rather than by design, and margin is quietly spent twice every time a job crosses the boundary between Groundwork Services and North East Tippers.

Build a reporting layer that matches the scale

At £47m turnover with 173 staff, the business has outgrown a structure where three family directors are the only management layer. Formal reporting that surfaces risk to margin and timeline early means problems show up while they are still cheap to fix, instead of arriving as surprises on site.
