The three structural changes most likely to move the needle

Top 3

Not solutions, the changes themselves. On the diagnostic Medhya confirms which of these are live for you, on your leaders' own answers, and what each one is worth.

Set clear decision rights

Give your project and contract managers defined authority to decide on site without routing back up. Decisions stop queueing behind the owner-directors and the ops desk, and senior time stops being spent on calls that should sit a level down on each fit-out.

Standardise how work flows between functions

One agreed way a job hands over from survey to install to commissioning across cabling, jointing, containment and data. The same work stops being redone two or three times before it is signed off, which is where margin is quietly spent twice on a fit-out.

Make leadership information formal, not informal

Reporting that surfaces risk to margin and programme early across every live site, rather than leaders relying on personal chasing to know what is really happening on each job. Problems show up while they are still cheap to fix.
